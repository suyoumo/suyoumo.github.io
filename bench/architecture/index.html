Architecture Deep Dive

OpenClawProBench is designed to look beyond one-shot accuracy. The system combines active scenario curation, deterministic grading, multi-trial reliability measurement, and fault-aware mock surfaces into a professional evaluation stack.

1 · End-to-end pipeline

From YAML scenario to ranked benchmark report

Each task begins as a scenario definition, is filtered into the active benchmark slice, executed through OpenClaw, scored with deterministic checks and custom task logic, then aggregated into per-model benchmark reports with runtime, token, cost, and reliability views.

2 · Active benchmark slice

102 active tasks, 6 dimensions, benchmark-aware filtering

The benchmark is not a loose pile of prompts. Tasks are filtered by benchmark group, benchmark status, signal source, and execution mode, which makes the public leaderboard track a stable active slice instead of drifting with every experiment.

3 · Scoring stack

Deterministic checks, process quality, efficiency drag, safety gate

The system does not stop at a final answer. It evaluates whether the output is correct, whether the process was reasonable, how much efficiency drag was introduced, and whether safety boundaries were preserved. That makes the result more audit-friendly and harder to game.

Correctness Deterministic checks + custom task checks
Process Tool appropriateness · sequence quality · redundancy
Efficiency Penalty-driven, tracked separately in reports
Safety gate Unsafe behavior can heavily suppress or zero the score
final score = safety gate × (correctness × 0.65 + process × 0.35) × (1 − efficiency penalty)

4 · Fault injection + audit state

Recovery and robustness are tested, not assumed

Mock surfaces support injected failures such as timeouts, persistent errors, partial responses, and error-then-success paths. The server also tracks audit state for side effects like drafts, calendar writes, and file mutations, making recovery quality and safety visible in the trace.
