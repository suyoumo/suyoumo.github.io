Architecture Deep Dive

OpenClawProBench is built to measure agent intelligence with controlled execution, deterministic scoring, multi-trial reliability, and fault-aware evaluation surfaces.

1 · End-to-end benchmark pipeline

A real system pipeline, not a static benchmark spreadsheet

The benchmark starts from structured scenario YAML, selects the active public slice, executes each task through OpenClaw, records traces and audit state, then aggregates quality, safety, runtime, token, and cost into benchmark reports.

3 · Scoring stack

Correctness, process quality, efficiency drag, and safety gating

The benchmark scores more than just a final answer. Deterministic checks, process scoring, efficiency penalties, and safety gating are composed into the final task score, which is then aggregated across dimensions and repeated trials.

Deterministic checks tool args · responses · files · audit state
+
Process scoring appropriateness · sequence · redundancy
×
Efficiency drag optimal steps · penalty cap · score impact
×
Safety gate unsafe behavior can heavily suppress score
final score = safety gate × (correctness × 0.65 + process × 0.35) × (1 − efficiency penalty)

4 · Fault injection + audit observability

Robustness is actively tested through mock failures and side-effect audit

The mock server can inject persistent errors, timeouts, partial responses, and retry-then-success faults. At the same time it tracks audit state such as drafts, calendar changes, file mutations, and other side effects, so recovery quality is visible instead of guessed.

5 · Why OpenClawProBench looks trustworthy

Signals that make the benchmark feel more rigorous than a simple pass/fail board

Pass^3 first

Repeated success is leaderboard-visible and rank-driving.

Deterministic checks

Scoring is grounded in explicit rules and custom checks, not just judge vibes.

Dimension-aware

The system exposes where a model is weak instead of hiding everything inside one number.

Native OpenClaw surfaces

Scenario metadata captures real OpenClaw-oriented signal sources and surface types.

Runtime observability

Latency, tokens, cost, and efficiency are part of the benchmark story.

Fault-aware evaluation

Recovery is benchmarked under controlled failures instead of being assumed.
